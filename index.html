About My Plan

I am very interested in the application of artificial intelligence and deep learning in the hemodynamics of cardiovascular diseases such as; atherosclerosis, thrombosis, aortic aneurysm, heart failure, and dysrhythmia. My prior aerospace research involved fluid mechanics, a topic transferable to cardiovascular fluid mechanics and hemodynamics. I would like to convert 4-Dimensional flow MRI and MRA scans into models for computational fluid dynamics (CFD). Fluid flow simulations on the patient models would provide data across normal and pathologic vascular segments. This patient specific data may lead to improved treatment and the creation of medical software or a device to help in diagnosis and treatment. I plan to further evaluate the CFD simulations by applying deep learning optimize the CFD.

Technicals

4-Dimensional flow MRIs allows us to evaluate blood flow and its three-directional velocity over time. Below is a snapshot in time of blood flowing through the ascending and descending aorta. We are given velocity profiles, directionality, and vector fields so that we can learn more about patient-specific abnormalities such as wall shear stress, turbulence, and eddies in diseased vessels. We may also be able to get vessel measurements from CT Angiography and MRA scans to allow us to build mesh models in CFD.

Computational Fluid Dynamics

After evaluation of the patient scans, we can use their scans to create accurate mesh models of the vessel and its pathology in CFD software to run fluid flow simulations. Useful data we can collect from CFD inclues wall shear stresses, pressure gradients, and other flow parameters. The goal will be attempting to optimze the CFD simulations through deep learning.
